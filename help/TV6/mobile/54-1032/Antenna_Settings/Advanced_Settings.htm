Advanced Settings

The following modes are special features that enhance the performance of the TracVision antenna. You can enable/disable these modes at the web interface (go to Settings > Advanced Settings).

  • Sleep Mode: When you are moving, the antenna’s motors need to run continuously to keep the antenna pointed at the satellite and locked onto the peak signal. However, if you are stationary, the motors don’t need to run since the antenna’s orientation to the satellite doesn’t change. In these stationary conditions, Sleep mode locks the antenna in place and shuts down the motors to conserve power and silence the antenna. KVH recommends that you keep Sleep mode set to ON (default setting).

  • Sidelobe Mode: All parabolic dish antennas, including the TracVision antenna, form a radiation pattern that will pick up satellite signals consisting of a main beam at the center of the signal along with sidelobes a few degrees away. In most cases, the received signal does not generate sidelobes strong enough to interfere with the main beam. However, in the rare cases where they do, less advanced antennas will sometimes mistake a sidelobe for the main beam, resulting in low signal strength and dropped reception. With Sidelobe mode turned on, the TracVision antenna will sweep across the area of the satellite and look for a stronger signal during acquisition to ensure it is tracking the main beam. Due to the infrequent need, KVH recommends that you keep Sidelobe mode set to OFF (default setting), unless directed otherwise by KVH Technical Support.
